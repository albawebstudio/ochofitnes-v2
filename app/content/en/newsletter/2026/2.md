### A Note From Me

I took this picture when the kids were here. The first word, "**esperar**" means to wait. The
second word, "**renacer**" means to be reborn.

"**Esperar**" is a hard word. When the kids left, the house felt very quiet. I was walking with them
one day when I saw this mural, and it hit me — a reminder that I have to wait a little while longer
before I see them again.

So what have I been doing to stay sane?

I finished the training program I wrote for January. Since I was off most of December, I had to
create a modified program for myself. I've also started taking classes with different coaches —
learning new approaches and sharpening my skills. I go for late-night runs when my mind won't
slow down thinking about the kids. I even started taking dance classes. I can't wait to dance
with my girls again.

Nothing feels quite the same when the kids aren't here. But in the meantime, I have to buck up.

### Starting Over — With Experience

Moving to Mexico meant starting from zero in many ways. But I want you to know something
about me.

I've had **two ankle surgeries** where surgeons told me I would never be able to run or exercise
again. I've spent months on crutches, figuring out how to move safely. I've injured my back at
work, severely hurt my shoulder, torn ligaments in my ankle, and torn a muscle in my leg.

Every injury and setback has taught me something. It's allowed me to truly walk in my clients'
shoes — to understand frustration, fear, and patience — and to gain the knowledge needed to
help people train safely and prevent injuries.

That experience matters.

## If You're Starting Again — That's Okay

Maybe you're starting your health journey again. That's okay. We get many chances in life and
fitness to begin again — but the key is that we keep going.

The words in this picture are a good reminder: wait… and be made new. We should come out of
tough situations — and tough training programs — better than before.

Let's give this year a stronger version of ourselves.

Life is beautiful, even when it's hard.

I know many of you have gone through unexpected medical challenges. I can imagine how
challenging it was to receive that news — and how hard it was for your families to watch you suffer.
But guess what?

You're savages.
You're still here.
You're getting through it.

We don't give up. We're not quitters. We keep going — for ourselves and for the people who love
us.

## This Month's Question

### Can I get stronger and change my body with online training?

Absolutely. Science says **yes**.

A recent study compared three training approaches:

1. **Supervised training** (online coaching in real time)
2. Unsupervised training using video recordings
3. Unsupervised training using a written program

### The winner? Option 1.

"Supervised training wasn't just effective — it was the most effective option in this particular study."

Participants showed improvements in resting heart rate, cardiovascular fitness, and strength.
   
Source: [The Manual-Remote Personal Training Study](https://www.themanual.com/fitness/study-shows-remote-personal-training-effectively-boosts-fitness/)
